HOW TO PLAY


NUMBER OF PLAYERS  2


ORIGIN
Pallankuzhi (பல்லாங்குழி), also Pallam Kuzhi, Pallangulli, Pallankuli or Pallanguzhi is one of the age-old South Indian Traditional Games played by Tamils in Southern India and Sri Lanka.


Pal means in Tamil "many" and kuzhi means "pit" and could literally be translated as many pits, a fitting description of the mancala boards used to play mancala games.

BENEFITS
It’s an indoor game which helps us to do mind calculations,hand-eye coordination and also helps us to increase our concentration and patience.

The game is popular among children, women and older people.


OBJECTIVE
The major objective of the Pallankuzhi game is to capture more stones than the opponent.


RULES

  1. The Pallankuzhi board has 14 holes, each player controlling one row of seven.
  2. Initially five seeds, shells or small stones are put in each hole.
  3. On his turn a player lifts the seeds from any of his holes and, going counter-clockwise, distributes one seed into the following holes.
  4. After a player has dropped his last seed, he takes the seeds from the next hole and distributes them.
  5. If the last seed falls into a hole with an empty hole beyond, the seeds in the hole following the empty hole are captured by the player and placed into his store.
  6. Play is continued if the next hole contains seeds. If the last seed falls into a cup, which is followed by two empty holes, nothing is captured and the turn is over.
  7. If, after having a seed dropped into it, a hole contains four seeds, those are captured by the player whose side the hole is.
  8. The next player continues play in the same way.
  9. If a player has no seeds left to make a move, the round is over and his opponent captures the remaining seeds.
  10. The winner will have maximum number of seeds which are kept in his store.

In this digitized version of game, a player is only allowed to choose the hole and the steps 3 to 7 are done automatically and the changes are not visible.
